Preview: Sharks vs. L.A. Kings
October 22nd, 2015

The Puck Times is a concisely curated e-mail newsletter for Sharks fans. We scan the news and synthesize it for you, every morning before the puck drops. Find past issues at pucktimes.com/archives.

NEED TO KNOW
Game details: Sharks vs Los Angeles Kings, puck drops 7:30pm

  • After opening the season with four straight wins, the Sharks have dropped their last two games against the New York Islanders and New York Rangers. San Jose lost both contests by multiple goals, a 6-3 loss vs the Islanders and a 4-0 loss against the Rangers.

  • The injury bug has landed on the Sharks early this season with center Logan Couture out with a broken leg (out 4-6 weeks), defenseman Paul Martin out with an undisclosed lower-body injury, and forwards Ben Smith and Joonas Donskoi have also been sidelined recently.  

  • Thursday’s game with the Kings marks San Jose’s return home after a 2-2 East coast road trip.

  • After the Sharks thrashed the Kings on opening night in Los Angeles, the Kings continued to struggle in their next two game losing to Arizona 4-1 and Vancouver 3-0. However, LA has bounced back over their last two games, winning a pair of tight 2-1 contests over Minnesota and Colorado.

IN CASE YOU MISSED IT: Game Recap: Sharks Blanked by Rangers 4-0

  • The Sharks were shutout by Rangers’ backup goaltender Antti Raanta who was making his season debut. Raanta only made 22 saves, but a number of them were Grade-A stops.

  • Matt Nieto was one of the few Sharks forwards with any jump as he got bumped up to the top line with Joe Thornton and Joe Pavelski. Despite being much maligned by a number of Sharks fans for his scoring woes last season, Nieto has often been a sparkplug for this team and for the top line. Last season when Tomas Hertl struggled with the Joe's, Nieto took over and gave that line some much needed speed.

  • Martin Jones suffered his first loss as a Shark in the road trip finale against the Rangers. Neither of the first three goals could be blamed on the goaltender and the fourth was still no gimme. Jones has yet to be beat straight up without a tip or screen and still sports a .957 save percentage.

  • San Jose gave up far too many odd man rushes in this contest and that is concerning with defensman Paul Martin still out of the lineup. 

Who's Hot & Who's Cold?

  • Joe Pavelski and Joe Thornton share the Sharks team lead with five points through six games.

  • Patrick Marleau leads the Sharks with three goals and Joel Ward has four points in six games.

  • Defenseman Justin Braun is a plus-5 to lead the Sharks through six games.

  • Brent Burns and Brenden Dillon have not looked good together as a defensive pair with Martin out, and can be considered on a "cold streak." Burns has registered a minus-4 and Dillon a minus-3 over the past two games.

  • Tyler Toffoli has scored in each of LA’s back-to-back wins heading into Thursday’s game with the Sharks. He leads the Kings in scoring with three goals and no assists.

  • Anze Kopitar scored the OT winner two games ago, but is otherwise off to a slow start to the season. Both he and defenseman Drew Doughty have just one point each through the first five games of the season.
