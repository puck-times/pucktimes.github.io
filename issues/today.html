Preview: Sharks @ New York Rangers

October 19th, 2015

The Puck Times is a concisely curated e-mail newsletter for Sharks fans. We scan the news and synthesize it for you, every morning before the puck drops. Find past issues at pucktimes.com/archives.

WHAT YOU NEED TO KNOW

Game details: Sharks @ New York Rangers, puck drops at 4:00pm.
 

  • The Sharks suffered their first defeat of the season Saturday night in Brooklyn against the NY Islanders, losing by a final score of 6-3. Tonight's match up against the Rangers marks the finale of this 4-game east-coast road trip. The Sharks will then return home to face the LA Kings this Thursday at 730pm.
  • While the Sharks continue to lead the Pacific Division with a 4-1-0 record, the road trip has proven costly on the injury front. Star forward Logan Couture will miss at least 4-6 weeks with a fractured fibula, while less serious injuries to Paul Martin, Joonas Donskoi, and Ben Smith have forced head coach Peter DeBoer to creatively manage lines and call up some fresh faces.
  • The Rangers enter tonight's contest looking to snap a 3-game losing streak.  After coming out strong to start the season 3-0 and outscoring their opponents 12-6, the Rangers have mustered just two goals in their last 3 games.
  • Rangers to watch: In addition to superstar Rick Nash, the Sharks will need to keep an eye on the Rangers' young talent tonight. Rookie Oscar Lindberg has dazzled in his first six games, and leads the Rangers offensively with 4 goals and 5 points to start the season. Additionally, watch out for Kevin Hayes, the Rangers 6'5, big-bodied winger, who has impressed with his hustle and puck possession thus far in his sophomore season. 

 

IN CASE YOU MISSED IT - Sharks Lose 6-3 to Islanders

  • Alex Stalock had a strong first period in his first action of the season, stopping all 10 shots including a penalty shot by Mikhail Grabovski.
  • In the second period, however, things began to get out of hand. Johnny Boychuk beat Stalock with a perfect slapper from the side boards 21 seconds into the 2nd period. Minutes later, the Islanders' scored on a controversial non-call. After a shot from the point snapped a buckle on his face mask, Stalock immediately pulled his mask off, which normally triggers an immediate whistle. However, the official rule allows for an "impending scoring opportunity" to be completed before the play is blown dead, and Anders Lee took advantage by banking the puck off of Stalock from behind the net to tie the game at 2-2.
  • The Sharks resiliently bounced back after the Islanders' first two goals. Rookie Nikolay Goldobin re-established the 2-1 lead just 20 seconds after Boychuk's goal, earning his first career NHL goal on a classic beauty of a setup by Joe Thornton.  
  • Midway through the second period with the score 2-2, Matt Nieto showed off his speed and finesse with an equally beautiful setup to Joel Ward, whose first goal of the season made it 3-2 Sharks. 
  • From there, the Islanders would go on to score 4 unanswered goals, as defensive mistakes and soft goaltending allowed the Islanders to pull away. A turnover by Burns led to Josh Bailey's equalizer to make it 3-3. Early in the 3rd, Kyle Okposo made it 4-3 after corralling his own rebound off the boards and driving to the net relatively uncontested, where a simple poke check by Stalock should have neutralized the threat. Finally, Brock Nelson made it 5-3 with a seemingly innocent wrist shot from between the circles, before an empty netter finished the game at 6-3.
  • The Sharks were outshot 34-25 for the first time this season, as the Islanders dictated the pace of play for the final 30 minutes.
  • For the third straight game, we witnessed a stoppage for video review, as Islanders' coach Jack Capuano used his coach's challenge to unsuccessfully allege goalie interference on Pavelski's first-period goal.

PRE-GAME SKATE 

  • After finishing last season with the best record in the NHL and losing a heart-breaking game 7 in the Eastern Conference finals, the Rangers enter 2015 with the highest of expectations.
  • As such, frustration is starting to brew in Manhattan as the Rangers put in a lackluster effort against the lowly New Jersey Devils last night, losing by a score of 2-1 in OT. With tonight's game being the second of back-to-back's, it will be key for the Sharks to get all four lines rolling early and take advantage of their fresher legs.
  • Rangers star left wing Rick Nash is still looking for his first goal of the season, after leading the team with 42 goals last year.
  • While the offense has struggled, goaltender Henrik Lundqvist has looked stellar in starting all six games so far this season. He has posted a 2.16 goals-against and a .931 save percentage. Given the back to back scenario tonight, coach Alain Vigneault will likely rest Lundqvist and give Antti Raanta his first start of the year.

SHARKS STARTING LINEUP

Ever since Andrew Bensch commented on the tremendous depth of the Sharks roster last week, the injury hits have kept piling up. To recap, the Sharks have lost Logan Couture for 4-6 weeks due to a broken fibula sustained in practice. Both Joonas Donskoi and Paul Martin missed Saturday's game due to undisclosed lower body injuries, ostensibly both from blocking shots. Then on Saturday against the Islanders, Ben Smith left the game after being struck in the ear by a puck in the 2nd period. While the Sharks have not commented on Smith's condition, all signs point to concussion protocol holding him out a few games, and sources report that center Bryan Lerg has been called up from the Barracuda to take his place. Likewise, we expect to see Dylan DeMelo again tonight as the Sharks 6th defensemen in place of Martin, and for Goldobin to continue flank the top line while Donskoi is out.
