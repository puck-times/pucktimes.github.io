NEED TO KNOW
Game details: Sharks vs Carolina Hurricanes, puck drops 730pm

  • After opening the season with four straight wins, the Sharks have dropped their last three in a row, all by three goals or more. They lost to the Islanders 6-3, the Rangers 4-0 and the Kings, 4-1. 
  • The injury bug has landed on the Sharks early this season with center Logan Couture out with a broken leg (out 4-6 weeks), defenseman Paul Martin out with an undisclosed lower-body injury, and forwards Ben Smith and Joonas Donskoi have also been sidelined recently.  
  • Fortunately as far as injuries go, Martin, Smith, and Donskoi are back practicing and Melker Karlsson, last year's rookie sensation has started playing for the Barracuda.
  • The Hurricanes are predictably off to a slow start (2-4 going into Friday) as they are expected to be a bottom feeder in the Eastern Conference this season.
  • That said, the Sharks struggled against these types of opponents last season, particularly against the Hurricanes, losing at home last year and only winning in Carolina thanks to the NHL debut heroics of goaltender Troy Grosenick.
  • Carolina  won/lost last night in Los Angeles so tonight's game in San Jose will be the second of back-to-backs for them, a game San Jose should win to snap this losing skid.
IN CASE YOU MISSED IT: Game Recap: Sharks Fall 4-1 to Kings
  • With Martin missing his third straight game, the Sharks once again struggled defensively, giving up far too many odd man rushes. Brent Burns was guilty of leaving defense partner Brenden Dillon out to dry on LA's first goal, a pretty tally by Tyler Toffoli who undressed former teammate Martin Jones with a nifty move on a 2-on-1 rush. Later in the game, Jeff Carter also scored on a 2-on-1, beating Jones through the five hole to make it 3-0.
  • San Jose's penalty kill has started struggle, giving up two power play goals to the Kings, although the second of which was just a terribly unlucky bounce off the end boards that carromed in off Jones who had yet to locate the puck after the initial shot went wide.
  • The Sharks mixed up their lines despite opening up the game with 18 shots in the first period. Joel Ward scored the Sharks lone goal on a new look top line with Joe Thornton and Joe Pavelski.
 

Who's Hot & Who's Cold?

  • Pavelski leads the Sharks with six points through seven games.
  • Ward has been terrific in every game so far, and now has five points.
  • Thornton is San Jose's leader in goals for per 60 minutes of ice time.

  • Brent Burns and Brenden Dillon remain on a cold streak as a defensive pair, with continued struggles against L.A.

  • Hurricanes forwards Eric Staal and Kris Versteeg share the team lead with four points (going into Friday) along with defenseman Justin Faulk

  • Other notable names for Carolina, Jordan Staal and Elias Lindholm have combined for just one point between them (going into Friday)
