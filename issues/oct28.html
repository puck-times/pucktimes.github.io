Preview: Sharks vs. Nashville Predators
October 28th, 2015

The Puck Times is a concisely curated e-mail newsletter for Sharks fans. We scan the news and synthesize it for you, every morning before the puck drops. Find past issues at pucktimes.com/archives.

NEED TO KNOW
Game details: Sharks vs Nashville Predators, puck drops 730pm.

  • Through eight games, the Sharks are off to a solid 5-3 start. After winning their first four games, they dropped three in a row before rebounding last game with a 5-2 win over Carolina (more on that below). 
  • Finally some positive news on the injury report - last game saw the return of defenseman Paul Martin after he missed the previous three games (maybe not so coincidentally all three losses). Additionally, Joonas Donskoi, Ben Smith and Melker Karlsson are all seemingly close to being available again for the big club.
  • With a number of teams off to great starts like the Montreal Canadiens (9-0-0), Dallas Stars (7-2-0) and the New York Islanders (6-2-1), the Nashville Predators aren't getting the attention they deserve. They are also out to a fantastic start at (6-1-1), and are tied for second in the league in goals against with just 16.
  • Historically, San Jose has had good success against the Predators, and last year they took the season series with a 2-1-0 record. Both wins were 2-0 shutouts on home ice.
  • Both the Sharks and the Predators last played Saturday night, so all legs should be fresh for what ought to be an exciting hockey game between two top caliber teams.
IN CASE YOU MISSED IT: Game Recap: Sharks Beat Carolina 5-2
  • Saturday night in San Jose featured a three act performance by new fan favorite Joel Ward. The first-year Sharks scored two first period goals to stake the Sharks to an early two-goal lead and then he finished off the hat trick with an empty netter in the third period.
  • While it is worth noting the Sharks lost the shot battle 34-21, they were playing the vast majority of the game with a lead, and a two-goal lead at that for most of the night. Therefore, even though the Hurricanes carried the play for stretches, it isn't all that concerning. San Jose got their chances and buried them.
  • Tommy Wingels picked up his first goal of the season and Joe Pavelski rounded out the scoring for the Sharks.
  • Martin Jones was once again solid in net, stopping 32 of those 34 Carolina shots for a nice bounce back game for him after a couple of so-so outings against New York and Los Angeles.
 

Who's Hot & Who's Cold?

  • The Reggae man Joel Ward now has five goals in his last four games and leads the team in points with eight.
  • Joe Pavelski's goal last game gives him seven points in eight games to start the season.
  • After scoring three goals in his first four games, Patrick Marleau has gone cold with no goals and just a single assist in his last four games, while accumulating a minus-6 rating in that span.
  • Former Pittsburgh Penguins star James Neal already has six goals and nine points to lead the Predators in scoring.
  • In seven games, Nashville's stud goaltender Pekka Rinne is sporting a .932 save percentage. Over the last two games he has stopped 50 of 53 shots for a .943 save percentage.
